Academics

Rules & Regulations

General Guidelines:

  • Students need to attend school regularly and punctually.
  • Every student will bring his/her school diary, daily and without fail.
  • Students who come to school in their own transport should not reach before 7.25 am. School gates will be open in the morning at 7.25 am and closed at 8.00 am.
  • The bell is the signal for all to go to their respective classrooms. They should enter classrooms silently without jostling and pushing.
  • The school uniform is to be worn on all school days and at all school functions. Students should not be shabbily dressed.
  • Care should be taken not to disfigure or damage school property. Any loss or damage to school property due to wanton destruction/carelessness of a student will lead to the imposition of fine and recovery of cost.
  • No books (other than text or library books), magazines, or periodicals may be brought to the school without the Principal’s permission.
  • Children are not allowed to bring valuables (gold, precious ornaments, electrical gadgets, or cash, etc.) to the school. The school is not responsible for articles/personal belongings lost by the students.
  • Parents should fill up the record for Non-Attendance for each day the student is absent from school, stating the reasons for his/her absence.
  • Parents/Guardians are not allowed to meet their wards or the teachers in classrooms during school hours. Prior permission is to be sought from the Principal in case of emergencies.
  • Parents are required to inform the school if there is any change in their address and/or telephone numbers immediately.
  • No student is allowed to leave the class or school premises during school hours without the permission of the class teacher and the Principal.
  • The school insists on the maintenance of strict discipline. Any child who violates the set rules of the school discipline will not be allowed to attend the school. The Principal reserves the right to dismiss any student without assigning any reason.

Attendance:

  • The first bell rings at 7:50 A.M. and all students are required to be in school by/before the first bell. At 7:50 roll call is marked by the class teacher in the home room period.
  • The school gate is closed at 8:00 a.m. and no latecomers are allowed in the school premises after this time.
  • It is compulsory for all students to attend the morning assembly.
  • At every school program/activity/function as applicable, attendance is compulsory.
  • 75% attendance is compulsory for promotion.
  • There is a prize for full attendance awarded at the time of the annual function.
  • Withdrawal of child during school hours for attending religious/social functions is not allowed. In case of emergency, Principal may be contacted.
  • No leave of absence is granted except for compelling/unavoidable reasons subject to a written application submitted well in advance.
  • Absence for more than five days after vacations renders the student liable to have his/her name struck-off from the rolls.

Examination:

  • Pre Primary Assessment: Non-formal evaluation system in the form of Class Assessment for students of Pre Primary and 3 Rounds of Internal Evaluation Worksheets for classes Pre School to II form the evaluation system.
  • Pupil Evaluation: Consists of Two Parts.
  • Evaluation for Class III onwards will be carried out as per the guidelines of CBSE under the Continuous Comprehensive Evaluation System (CCE).
  • Mock / Pre-Board Examination: These exams are administered to classes X and XII to give students practice in taking exams of the entire course on the board pattern.
  • Eligibility: A student is required to have 75% attendance to be eligible to appear for the Annual Examination. In genuine cases, the Principal may condone the shortage in attendance not exceeding 10%.
  • Promotion Criteria: Promotion is made on the basis of cumulative results of Weekly tests in each subject and comprehensive examination.
  • The decision of the school authorities regarding promotion is final and binding on the parents/guardian. No student is given double promotion.
  • CCE (Continuous and Comprehensive Evaluation): As per CBSE guidelines, at the end of class X, all students are issued CCE Certificates. The CCE Certificate includes Academic Performance, Personal & Social Qualities, Attitudes & Values, Co-Curricular Activities, Outdoor Activities, Health status, and Attendance of a child. The Certificate will bear the grading based on the evaluation of the student during his/her stay in the school up to Class X. The Academic Performance will be a record of Marks obtained by the child in Class IX and X in two terminal exams.

Assessments

Assessments play a key role in the academic journey at Vishal Bharti School. We conduct periodic evaluations to assess student performance and progress. Here are the key details of our assessment system:

Internal Evaluation System

(i) L.K.G., U.K.G., and II:

Students will be evaluated through their everyday work, class response, participation in various co-scholastic activities, and writing skills. Only worksheets will be given for evaluation, and the evaluation will be divided into two terms.

(ii) Classes: III-V

Evaluation for these classes is divided into two terms. Along with periodic tests, internal assessments will be taken. Internal assessment will be based on the following components:

  • Language skills (reading, speaking, listening, and writing)
  • Project work
  • Notebook submission
  • Holiday homework
  • Activities, quizzes, OTBA (Open Text-Based Assessment)
  • Portfolio
  • Subject enrichment

(iii) Classes: VI-XII

The examination pattern for classes XI and XII will be spread over the whole year with unit tests, half-yearly, and annual examinations, each consisting of 80 marks. Marks are allocated for internal assessments, which will contribute 20 marks and will be added to the unit test, OTBA, projects, portfolios, notebooks, subject enrichment activities, quizzes, and pre-board examinations. A minimum of 75% attendance is required for students to be allowed to appear in the Annual Examination.

Subject Options for Class XI

To get admission in class XI, students need to select one of the following streams. The selection of subjects will depend upon the grade and percentile rank secured in the final result of class X.

1. SCIENCE STREAM
  • Medical: English, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Maths, Information Practices, Physical Education.
  • Engineering: English, Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Maths, Computer Science, Physical Education.
2. COMMERCE STREAM (With Maths)
  • English, Maths, Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics, Physical Education.
3. COMMERCE STREAM (Without Maths)
  • English, Information Technology/Physical Education, Accountancy, Business Studies, Economics.
4. ARTS/HUMANITIES STREAM
  • English Core, Hindi Elective, Political Science, Economics, Sociology, Physical Education.
